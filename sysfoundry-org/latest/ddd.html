Domain Orientation

The below diagram provides the domain / subdomain breakdown of a typical enterprise software system.

Here Domain / Sub-Domain means "A specified sphere of activity or knowledge."

Business / Problem Domain

The Business or problem domain is the area of knowledge / activity which is the primary focus of a software developer / team. For example the following are some of possible domains applied to specific narrow domains such as Travel and Transport, ECommerce, etc.

  1. Accounting

  2. Revenue Management

  3. Logistics and Supplychain

and so on…​

System Domain

The system domain is the area of knowledge / activity which are concerned with how the solution to a business problem is supported by generic domains such as the below mentioned

  1. Security

  2. Monitoring

  3. Management & Operations etc.

Where does Sysfoundry fit in?

In the above mentioned domain oriented view, the Sysfoundry provides the below capabilities to address some of the domain / sub-domain specific concerns.

  1. A SDK & Framework to enable software developers / teams to build solutions to Business Problem Domains.

  2. A set of services packaged and tested to address some of the domain / sub-domain support services such as Identity Management, Access Management, Monitoring etc.
